Space Rebound

My first finished Arcade Game — a lesson in beginnings and scope

Overview Gallery Technical
Arcade Game Solo Project Unity + C# 2024

Overview

A game about ricochet mechanics was an idea stuck in my head for multiple years, so when I started studying media informatics and I had to make my first game, I decided to finally put this idea into practice in a small, simple space-themed Arcade Game.

Mechanically, the game is quite simple. The player character (a triangular sprite representing a spaceship) can be moved left or right either via the arrow keys or via the "A" and "D" keys respectively. The triangle always faces towards the mouse and the player can shoot bullets by pressing the left mouse button. The bullets bounce off walls, becoming stronger and changing their colour. For each bounce they go from white, to blue, to orange. Enemies (custom spaceship sprites) come in these three colours as well, which signals how much damage they deal, how strong a bullet needs to be at least to hurt them, and how many points they award the player for defeating them. After reaching specific score counts, the player reaches the next level (without disrupting any gameplay). With every new level, more enemies and stronger enemies appear on average, until the final level is reached and the game goes on until the player is defeated, saving their score in a local leaderboard.

This was probably the least problematic development process I have had so far, mostly because this project worked even with quite a small scope of features. I was able to continually build the project over a few months, one step at a time, without needing to change direction or refocus my vision. It started humble, it stayed humble, and it was an amazing project to teach me the fundamentals of game development in Unity.

Looking at the project now, I still see areas that could be improved upon. More enemies and more interesting enemies would make the gameplay loop a bit more exciting and would open the door for more unique level configurations with specific enemy types that have synergies or more complex gameplay interactions. In the same breath, the enemy balancing would need another look, as the core ricochet mechanic makes stronger enemies exponentially harder to defeat, while the weakest enemy type can be defeated even without ricocheting bullets, making them feel more like an afterthought.

Technical Details & Systems

Architecture & Implementation

My original vision proved to be a perfect challenge to learn the basics of Unity, like how to translate the player's mouse cursor into a rotation that makes the player character look at the cursor position at all times, or how to instantiate objects, giving them behaviour and a complete working lifecycle without interfering with the game's physics. I also dabbled a bit with particle systems and learned how to use custom fonts and music.

Engine

Unity 2022+ with C#

Gameplay Systems

2D physics for bullet behaviour, collision detection, simple enemy behaviours, saving data between gameplay sessions

Team Size

Solo Developer

Key Challenges & Solutions

The development process of this game was quite obstacle-free for the most part. I had some trouble with my custom font though, because the default font settings made it look blurry, which is quite bad for an 8-bit font. After a day of research, even this was easily solved.

What I Learned

The biggest lesson this project taught me was keeping the scope small and reasonable. It felt great working on this project because I could actually reach the goals I set. Additionally, it was my first deep dive into Unity and C#, and it was a lot of fun getting to know the workflow. In the end, I was holding my first fully functional, finished game in my hands, and I am quite happy with the result.
